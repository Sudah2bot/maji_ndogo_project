#  Maji Ndogo: Integrated Water Security & Drought Strategy

##  Executive Summary
Maji Ndogo is a region in crisis, where over 27 million people rely on old or contaminated water infrastructure. This project serves as a strategic roadmap for the Ministry of Water. By integrating survey data, pollution reports, and infrastructure costs, I have developed a prioritized intervention plan to secure clean water access for everyone in Maji Ndogo.

##  Strategic Impact
Based on my multi-table analysis of 60,000+ records, these are the prioritized actions required:

| Strategic Action | Target Group | People Impacted | Priority Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Repair** | Broken Taps in Home | 3,799,720 | 🔴 Critical |
| **Sanitize** | Contaminated Wells | 3,472,746 | 🔴 Critical |
| **Infrastructure** | River Users (Drought Risk) | 2,362,544 | 🟡 High |
| **Optimization** | Overcrowded Shared Taps | 11,945,272 | 🟢 Medium |

## Financial Roadmap
The total budget required to end the Maji Ndogo water crisis is **Ksh 77,758,500**.

- **Ksh 46,987,400** for Drilling Wells (Drought Mitigation for River users).
- **Ksh 28,721,500** for advanced filtration (RO/UV) in contaminated areas.
- **Ksh 2,049,600** for localized infrastructure diagnosis and tap repair.

##  The Tech Stack
- **Engine:** Python 3.13 (Pandas, Numpy)
- **Environment:** WSL (Ubuntu) managed via `uv`
- **Visualization:** Matplotlib & Seaborn
- **Version Control:** Professional Git Workflow (Branching/Merging)
